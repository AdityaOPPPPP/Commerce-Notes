Business Studies – Chapter 1

Nature and Purpose of Business

1. Business – Concept

Business refers to an economic activity involving production or purchase and sale of goods & services to earn profit by satisfying human needs.

2. Types of Human Activities

  • Economic Activities: Aim to earn income (e.g. business, profession, employment).
  • Non-Economic Activities: Performed for love, sympathy or social service.
  • Difference: Economic gives monetary gain; Non-economic gives personal satisfaction.

3. Characteristics of Business

  • Deals with goods & services
  • Continuous activity
  • Profit motive
  • Involves risk
  • Requires investment

4. Objectives of Business

  • Economic: Profit, growth, innovation.
  • Social: Welfare of employees, consumers, community.
  • Human: Development of employees.
  • National: Economic growth, employment generation.

5. Role of Profit

Profit is the lifeblood of business – it ensures survival, growth, reputation, and innovation.

6. Classification of Business Activities

  • Industry: Production of goods & materials.
  • Commerce: Distribution of goods from producers to consumers.

7. Industry – Meaning & Types

  • Primary: Extractive & genetic (agriculture, mining).
  • Secondary: Manufacturing [analytical, synthetic, processing, assembly] & construction.
  • Tertiary: Services (transport, banking, insurance, warehousing, advertising).

8. Commerce

Commerce includes all activities that remove hindrances in the movement of goods — trade, transport, banking, insurance, warehousing, advertising.

  • Hindrance of Person - Trader
  • Hindrance of Place [Place Gap] - Transport
  • Hindrance of Time [Time Gap] - Warehousing
  • Hindrance of Exchange - Bank
  • Hindrance of Risk - Insurance
  • Hindrance of Knowledge - Advertising

9. Classification of Commerce

9.1 Trade

Trade involves the buying and selling of goods and services to satisfy human wants.

  • Internal Trade: Trade within a country
    • Wholesale Trade – Buying in bulk and selling to retailers
    • Retail Trade – Selling goods directly to consumers
  • External Trade: Trade between countries
    • Export – Selling goods to other countries
    • Import – Buying goods from other countries
    • Entrepot – Buying goods from one country and selling to another without processing

9.2 Aids or Auxiliaries to Trade

These are activities that support trade by removing obstacles in the movement of goods:

  • Transport – Moving goods from producer to consumer
  • Warehousing – Storing goods safely
  • Banking – Providing finance and credit
  • Insurance – Protecting goods from loss or damage
  • Advertising – Informing consumers about products

10. Business Risk

Possibility of inadequate profit or loss due to uncertainties like competition, demand, price changes, etc.

  • Nature: Uncertainty, unavoidable, linked with profit.
  • Causes: Natural, economic, human, physical, political.
